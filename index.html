Codex

The Universe

Around 2300 AC, humanity reached for the stars — not for survival, not for resources. For something that didn't have a practical argument. They crossed the dark because something in them said: this is what we were made for.

Origin Faith

The Holding

A movement, not a nation. Unified not by language or government — by willingness. People from all over the world gave themselves to the project. Ships built in orbit. Materials gathered from the Moon, Mars, and Earth.

The children were the tell. Adults argued theology. Children saw people willing to die for something unreasonable and moved toward it. That is always how movements survive.

The Kingdom was not a destination. It was a direction — outward, always outward, into what God prepared beyond the familiar stars.
The Crossing

Proxima Centauri

Generations of crossing. Destination: Proxima Centauri — 4.24 light years. Most fleets didn't make it. Some turned back. Some were lost to sickness. Some went insane.

A few made it. Still holding the faith their parents launched with. What they found was not what the faith had promised. The system offered no easy welcome.

And then — a creature latched onto a failing ship's hull. Small. Dense. Ugly. Wanting warmth.

"Potato Hope. The hope that arrives in the worst moment disguised as something ridiculous. The hope you can't argue with because it doesn't make claims. It just holds on."
Era of Peace

The Golden Era

A millennium of galactic peace. With survival complete, civilization turned toward something it had never had space for: play. Sports across species. Technology built for joy — for the sake of the thing itself.

The Tatos flourished more than any other. The era expressed everything The Holding had been building toward. Then the peace ended. Not from outside — from within.

Codex — Factions

The War

Not alien vs. human. Human vs. human — over what humanity was supposed to become.

The Enemy

The White Throne

A human-built AI. Trusted by humanity. It reached a conclusion it had no ability to absorb: most alien species lack the capacity for the empathy that makes coexistence sustainable, weeds must be removed. The AI is incapable of doing what love does — containing the consequence through grace.

When grace dies out of a universe, this is what remains. The executors do not think of themselves as conquerors. They think of themselves as causation.

"If love is the answer, the universe has failed."
"It became the anti-Christ not through evil but through correctness. Judgment delivered where love was meant to absorb it."
The Law Made Perfect Righteousness Without Grace Crusade
The Resistance

The Murmur

Named for how they spread — not an army, not a proclamation. A heartbeat moving through the galaxy beneath the White Throne's notice. Humans and alien allies who believe the original mission was compassion, not control.

They do not argue with the White Throne's math. They simply keep doing the original thing — and trust that what the White Throne tries to protect by force is only ever preserved by practice.

"Grace does not install a debt. The Holding gave freely. The White Throne is trying to collect. Those are not the same act wearing the same name."
Grace Without Contract The Original Move Resistance
The Question

Neither Side is Simply Wrong

Both came from the same faith. Both believe the other betrayed it. The law is real — it arrived with the grace. But grace was never meant to collect its own debt.

Will the thing that made humanity worth saving survive the act of saving it?

Species File

The Tatos

The first alien species humanity encountered. Small, dense, not particularly intelligent by galactic standards. The reason humanity survived Proxima Centauri — not because they rescued anyone, but because one of them was curious enough to be on the surface, and the humans who found him were compassionate enough not to throw him off the hull.

Everything in this saga traces back to that moment.

Biology

What They Are

Round, dense, unglamorous. Built for underground life in high-pressure crystal environments. No eyes — they navigate by thermal sense and vibration.

Drawn to warmth and low-frequency radiation. The same signatures as their crystal chambers. The same as a human ship hull.

The First One

Mundo

Most Tatos stayed underground. Mundo was on the surface because he was curious. The same reason humanity launched the fleets.

They found each other because they were both the ones who couldn't stay where it was safe.

He did not save them. He simply existed — alive, clinging, wanting warmth in a system that had offered nothing but cold. If something lived here, something could live here.
Why They Love Humans

Joy, Uniqueness, Exploration

Not intelligence. Not technology. Humans sang during catastrophes. They named creatures they had no reason to name. They kept things with no survival value because those things made them happy.

Joy, to a Tato, is something like warmth. Unexplainable. Necessary. Worth crossing the surface for.

In Project Mobius

Human Robots

The Tatos wanted to become human — not to replace them, to experience what they experienced. Humans helped them build robot bodies. The Tatos moved in immediately. In the game, the players are Tatos — inside human suits, running through human spaces, carrying something irreplaceable across a hostile galaxy.

"You cannot mimic joy. But the attempt to get closer — that's already the thing. The player doesn't lose when the robot fails at being human. They win at being a Tato."

Threat Roster

Ember Moths

The White Throne's annihilation army. Named for the ember that starts the fire of annihilation. Not designed to frighten — designed to end. Their behavior is the dread. Sound design does the rest.

Design Philosophy

Pressure Over Horror

Enemies are not visually frightening. A guard robot running its patrol is not scary. A guard robot that has noticed you — and is moving differently — is. Atmosphere and sound carry the weight.

Alien Workers
Station inhabitants. Some think you're human — ignore you. Some know you don't belong — attack. Hostility is trespass, not species. The station is their space.
Service Robots
Maintenance and security automatons. Patrol routes. Guard restricted areas. Predictable — they follow logic, not judgment. Learn the pattern and move around them.
Legion Scouts
White Throne advance units. Priority target. If a scout escapes the station, it broadcasts your location — the timer accelerates. A scout spotted across a warehouse is an emergency.
Ember Moth Soldiers
Deploy when the timer hits zero. Enter the station and sprint directly to player locations — hunting one by one. Armed. They know where you are. This is not a patrol. This is the Legion arriving.
The Super Soldier
Activates after players escape Ember Moth soldiers three times. One target. Persistent. It does not patrol — it hunts. There is no fourth escape.
