# Mundo
*A Silent Providence mini story.*
*Perspective: Mundo, close third person. He navigates by thermal sense and vibration — not sight. Everything is warmth, pressure, and sound.*

---

Mundo had never been above ground this long before.

Most Tatos didn't go above ground at all. The surface was cold and wrong and offered nothing a sensible creature would want. Below the surface was the crystal warmth — the steady amber glow that hummed at exactly the right frequency, the gentle heat that never asked anything of you, the deep familiar pressure of home. Everything good was underground. This was a fact so obvious that the Tatos had never bothered to state it.

Mundo had been above ground for three hours.

He was not sure why. He had gone up to investigate a vibration — something large and low-frequency, not like the crystals, not like anything — and then he had kept going, because the vibration had gotten stronger, and stronger things were more interesting than weaker things, and this was simply how Mundo worked.

The vibration was coming from something enormous parked on the surface.

Mundo had pressed himself against it.

It was the warmest thing he had ever felt.

The crystals underground were warm. This was warm the way the crystals were warm, but *more* — sustained and deep and thrumming with a frequency that settled somewhere behind his ribs and said: *here. stay here.* He had never felt anything say that before. He had felt the crystals say many things — *safe, familiar, home* — but not *here.* The crystals said home. This said something else.

Mundo stayed.

Above him, the enormous warm thing shuddered.

---

Inside the warm thing, which was a ship, which was called the *Tenacity*, the last seventeen survivors of Expedition Four were having a very bad time.

Mundo did not know this. Mundo did not know what a ship was, or an expedition, or a number larger than "many." He knew the hull was warm and that something on the other side of it was producing a very interesting vibration at irregular intervals, which suggested either mechanical distress or something alive.

He pressed closer.

The vibration spiked. Then dropped. Then spiked again.

*Interesting,* thought Mundo, in the way that Tatos thought things, which was less like words and more like a full-body lean in a direction.

He leaned in.

---

The hatch opened twenty minutes later.

Mundo felt the change in pressure before anything else — a gust of warmer air, interior-warm, carrying a smell completely unlike anything underground. He did not move. Moving seemed unwise when large things changed suddenly. He had learned this about the surface: it changed. You waited. Then you assessed the warmth situation.

The warm thing that came out of the hatch was vertical. It moved wrong — too high off the ground, unsteady, producing sounds at a frequency Mundo associated with alarm. Several more warm vertical things followed it. They smelled different from each other, which was interesting. The Tatos all smelled more or less the same.

One of the warm vertical things made a very loud sound and pointed at Mundo.

Mundo did not move. He was still assessing the warmth situation. The vertical things were warmer than the surface but less warm than the hull. Preliminary conclusion: hull was still best option.

The warm vertical things gathered around him.

Mundo, who had no concept of being looked at, experienced this as a change in thermal density — several warm bodies in close proximity, creating a kind of surrounded warmth that was, if he was honest, also quite good.

He pressed slightly harder against the hull.

One of the warm vertical things made a different sound. Lower. Slower. The alarm frequency dropped.

Then it did something extraordinary: it put part of itself against the hull, right next to Mundo.

Mundo felt the warmth of it — direct, immediate, the specific warmth of something alive. He had felt the crystals. He had felt the ship. This was neither of those things. This was warm in a way that had no precedent in any of his previous thermal experiences, and Mundo was not sure what to do about it, so he did the only sensible thing.

He stayed.

---

The debate, inside the *Tenacity*, lasted four minutes.

Throw it off: two votes.
Leave it: three votes.
Try to communicate with it: one vote.
Just... see what happens: eleven votes.

Eleven votes won.

Lordy was already moving before the vote finished.

---

What followed was, from Mundo's perspective, extremely confusing.

The warm vertical things had mostly stayed back. One of them — the one who had made the lower, slower sound; the one whose warmth felt more deliberate than the others — came close. Very close. Mundo assessed the warmth situation: close, warm, slightly elevated temperature, producing a very quiet high-frequency sound that Mundo associated with concentration. Fine. Good, actually. Warmth was warmth.

Then something cold touched him.

Mundo moved. Not far — the hull was still behind him, the hull was still best — but he shifted, decisively, away from the cold thing. Cold things on the surface were bad. This was established. He had been operating on this principle his entire life.

The cold thing followed him.

Mundo pressed harder against the hull. The cold thing touched him again — the same spot, deliberate, brief, then gone. Then a different spot. Then gone again.

He endured this. The warm vertical thing holding the cold thing was still warm, which was confusing. The cold thing was bad. The warm thing was good. Both were happening simultaneously. Mundo processed this for a long time and arrived at no useful conclusion, so he did what he always did when the data was unclear: he stayed. Staying was almost always the correct choice. He had stayed on the surface when everything in his body said to go underground, and that had led him here. Staying had a good track record.

The cold thing went away.

Several more cold things arrived.

Mundo bore it with dignity.

Eventually the cold things stopped. The warm vertical thing made a series of sounds at the other warm vertical things. They made sounds back. The sounds had a quality Mundo had not heard before — they had gone from alarm-frequency to something lower, slower, with more space in it. He did not know the word for relief. He felt its thermal signature anyway.

---

Then nothing happened for a while.

The warm vertical things moved around, doing warm-vertical-thing activities. Mundo stayed against the hull. The hull was still warm. The situation remained acceptable.

One of the warm vertical things drifted toward him. Not the one with the cold implements. A different one. This one was moving without the purposeful quality the others had — no concentrated high-frequency hum, no cold instruments. Just moving. Casually. In his direction.

It stopped next to him.

Then it put its forelimb against his side.

Not the deliberate touch of examination. Not the systematic cold-thing approach. Just — a forelimb, resting there. Warm. Still. Not doing anything.

Mundo waited for it to do something.

It did not do anything. It just stayed there.

He had been cold for a long time. He had found the hull, which was the best warm. He had found the vertical things, who were also warm. And now one of the warm vertical things had put itself against him with no apparent purpose at all. No examination. No cold implements. No alarm-frequency sounds.

Just warmth, offered for no reason Mundo could determine.

He pressed back, slightly, against the forelimb.

The warm vertical thing made a sound he had not heard before. Short. Surprised. Then a different sound — lower, longer, with a quality that Mundo had no framework for, had never encountered underground or on the surface, had not known was a thing that could exist.

He did not know the word for it. He just knew it was warm.

He stayed where he was.

---

Someone said it looked like a potato. Dr. Grace, who had been maintaining crew morale for four months by running game nights on a dying ship, said it looked like a Tato. As in Dr. Mundo. As in the character from the game they'd been playing since the third month. Ugly, round, unkillable, showing up where it had no business being.

The name stuck before anyone thought to object.

Mundo knew none of this. Mundo was having the best thermal experience of his life and was fully committed to continuing it indefinitely. The ship was the best warm. The vertical things were also warm. The surface was still terrible, but he no longer needed to leave it because the best warm was here, and here was where Mundo was, and this seemed like a good arrangement.

He had been cold for a long time, moving across the wrong surface toward a vibration he didn't understand.

He had found the right warm.

He was staying.

---

*When the examination was done and the crew was trying to figure out what to do next, it was Ko who pointed at Mundo and said: we should follow him. See where he came from.*

*Nobody had a better idea. Lordy said yes.*

*The Tenacity made landfall three days later, guided by Mundo's instinctive knowledge of the crystal chambers below — a path he traced not by intelligence, but by warmth. Ko was first out of the ship after Mundo. He had been waiting his whole career to explore something new, and here was something new, and it was leading him somewhere warm.*

*They found shelter, building material, a power source, and light.*

*They called the species Tatos. After the character. After the joke. Because on a ship full of people who had survived four months of catastrophe by playing games together, naming the alien after a video game character felt exactly right.*

*Mundo never went back underground. There was no point. He had found something warmer.*
