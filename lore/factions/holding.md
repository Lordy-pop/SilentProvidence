# The Holding
*Faction File — Silent Providence*

---

## Overview

**The Holding** was the origin movement of humanity's departure from Earth. It was not a nation, a state, or a formal government, but a faithful remnant unified by a single conviction: that humanity was made to seek the stars, and that the faith required to do so was stronger than any fear or calculation standing against it.

In the active war era (Era 5), the Holding no longer exists as a unified political or theological entity. It dispersed during the Golden Era (Era 6), branching out into the specialized Vocation Houses. Yet its inheritance remains the foundational root of the entire conflict.

---

## Theological Foundation

The Holding believed that the Kingdom of Heaven spoken of in scripture was not a static destination to be built on Earth, but a direction — outward, always outward, into the infinite dark God prepared beyond the familiar stars. 

* **Grace without Debt:** They believed that compassion must be given away freely, overflowing without expectation of return.
* **The Living Testimony:** Unlike Earth's hollowed institutions, the Holding did not argue for its righteousness. It simply existed, fully and visibly, leaving gifts of technology and research behind for those who accused them. Its completeness was its testimony.
* **The Name:** *To hold* — to remain faithful through the vast gap between the promise of a new home and the actual arrival.

---

## The Three-Part Origin

The Holding was forged from three distinct groups who shared the same ship for entirely different reasons:

1. **The Founder (Unknown Name):** An American Christian of the rebellious, disruptive strain. They provided the theological spine, the vision of the stars, and the conviction that the current earthly order was a hollow compromise.
2. **The Elon-Types:** Tech billionaires with messianic self-understandings. They provided the capital, the ship designs, and the orbital manufacturing infrastructure. They wanted to conquer the stars; the Holding gave their ambition a soul.
3. **The Displaced (Primarily Mexicans):** People whom the Earth’s economic systems had finished using. Having already lost their sovereignty, their land, and their homes, they had nothing left to protect. They provided the physical labor and the absolute commitment needed to launch into the unknown.

These three groups did not agree on their motives, but they agreed on the destination. Across the Crossing, their struggles became the crucible that forged the specialized Houses.

---

## Dispersal: The Golden Era

Upon arriving at Proxima Centauri and discovering the crystal chambers, the urgent need for a unified generation-fleet dissolved. During the Golden Era — a thousand years of peace and play — the Holding quietly dispersed. 

Rather than collapsing, the movement crystallized into the **Houses of Vocation** (such as the House of the Covenant, the House of Mastery, and the House of the Way). The unified fleet became a decentralized galactic civilization.

---

## The Tatos' Inheritance

While humanity divided itself into Houses and eventually fractured into the war between the White Throne and the Murmur, the inheritance of the Holding did not die. It found an unexpected keeper: **The Tatos**.

Because the Tatos are an incredibly long-living species, they experience time on a scale humans cannot. To the Tatos, the era of the Holding and the arrival of the first human ships represented their greatest **Joy**. While humans moved on to new structures, new arguments, and eventually a galactic crusade, the Tatos held onto the memory of the Holding's arrival. 

They became the closest living heirs to the Holding's original spirit — not because they understood the theology, but because they loved the gratuitous, joyful presence of the people who first stepped off the ships.
