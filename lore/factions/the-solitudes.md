# The Solitudes
*Faction File — Silent Providence*

---

## Overview

**The Solitudes** (sometimes referred to by the resistance as *The Wilderness*) represent a powerful alien faction in the active war era (Era 5). They do not align with the White Throne, the Murmur, or the Zealots. 

While the other factions fight over how to interpret the Covenant or govern the warp network, the Solitudes reject the entire human framework. They seek to dismantle the interconnected galactic system, sever all warp connections, and isolate every alien species in their own native stellar bubbles.

---

## Antecedents — The Jarn of HeighLunde

The Solitudes did not invent their argument. Generations earlier, in the Early Expansion, a single proud species lived it to the end: the **Jarn** of HeighLunde, who heard humanity's compassion as an invasion, refused the grace offered them, and were consumed by their own refusal. The Jarn never theorized the position — they only died inside it. The Solitudes are what the same cry becomes when it grows cold, deliberate, and galactic: *grace is a pathogen; leave us to our own law.* *(See [the Jarn](../aliens/the-jarn.md), [HeighLunde](../heighlunde.md).)*

---

## The Rejection of the Imago Dei

The core conflict between the Solitudes and humanity is theological and psychological. Humans are created in the image of God (*Imago Dei*), which gives them a structural, native capacity for faith, covenant, and sacrificial love. Even in the depths of space, humans can hold onto an unseen promise.

The Solitudes do not possess this baseline. Because they lack the *Imago Dei*, they experience the Covenant not as a promise, but as an unbearable weight of uncertainty. To their logical, survival-oriented minds:
*   **Covenant is Instability:** Committing to an unseen divine plan or a generational promise without immediate, calculable returns is a terrifying threat to their security.
*   **Love is a Defect:** Sacrificial love is not a virtue, but a dangerous biological vulnerability. expending resources to save a different species is calculated as a suicidal error that violates the laws of natural survival.

---

## The Pathogen of Grace

Before humanity arrived, the galaxy was quiet and amoral. Cruelty, predation, and the consumption of the weak were not sins — they were simply the natural state of things. No accounting was required.

When human compassion entered the galaxy, it acted as a moral pathogen. By showing grace, humans introduced the knowledge of the good. With that knowledge, the law of the ledger arrived, rendering the aliens accountable and subject to the White Throne's crusades and verdicts. 

The Solitudes did not ask for human grace, and they reject the debt it installed. They argue that the only way to escape the judgment is to remove the moral contagion entirely by returning the galaxy to its pre-contact state of isolation.

---

## Direct Action: Severance

Unlike the Zealots who build competitive fleets to fight the White Throne, or the Murmur who build decentralized networks, the Solitudes' primary method of warfare is **Severance**. 

They do not seek to control the Mobius Drives or capture warp gates; they seek to **destroy them**. The Solitudes target:
*   **Warp Gate Sabotage:** Collapsing the jump lanes that link the star systems.
*   **Mobius Link Disruption:** Scrambling the communication arrays that allow the White Throne's Messengers to coordinate.
*   **Planetary Isolation:** Deploying field generators that lock their home systems out of the warp entirely, retreating into the quiet dark.

In the campaign of *Project Mobius*, the Solitudes represent a volatile wild card — a faction that will gladly collapse a warp route, trapping both the player and the White Throne's forces in the cold void, to keep their sky untouched by human feet.
