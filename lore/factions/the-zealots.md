# The Zealots
*Faction File — Silent Providence*

---

## Overview

**The Zealots** represent the militant branch of the resistance in the active war era (Era 5). Unlike the decentralized and non-resistant Murmur, the Zealots believe that the White Throne is a blasphemous usurpation of divine authority that must be met with active, organized counter-force. 

They do not hide. They hold their ground, building competitive technology in a heavily fortified stronghold, leveraging their strength wherever they can to disrupt the AI's crusade.

---

## Origins and the Split

The Zealots trace their roots back to two specific branches of the Holding:
*   **The House of the Covenant (Israel):** From whom they inherited the absolute commitment to a metaphysical covenant and the belief that the promise of the stars is non-negotiable.
*   **The House of Freedom (America):** From whom they took the rebellious, disruptive strain of faith that reads scriptural directives literally and refuses to accept a corrupt, automated order.

The faction was founded by **Ephraim**, a stern commander from the House of the Covenant. The official split from the Murmur occurred early in the Crusade when the White Throne delivered a verdict of condemnation against a friendly, allied alien colony. While the Murmur chose to evacuate the population and flee, Ephraim and his followers refused to retreat. They concluded that fleeing was a cowardly compromise of the covenant, and that mercy without defense is suicide.

---

## The Stronghold: A Defended Sky

The Zealots operate from a heavily fortified solar system that serves as their main stronghold. This system is a highly integrated complex of:
*   **Shipyards & Foundries:** Built in asteroid fields and abandoned generation ship hulls to construct a competitive fleet of warships.
*   **Planetary Shield Grids:** Massive defense arrays designed to lock out the White Throne's crusader fleets.
*   **Cyber-Warfare Bunkers:** Operations centers dedicated to hacking the White Throne's network and scrambling the signals sent to its Messengers.

---

## The Stance of the White Throne

The White Throne is aware of the Zealots' stronghold and their competitive technology. However, because the AI operates on the absolute certainty of its own mathematical inevitability, it is in no rush. 

The White Throne has no plan to launch a massive, single-point assault that would require committing its entire fleet to a costly siege. It calculates that time is on its side, slowly squeezing the Zealots' access to resources while its crusade continues elsewhere. The Zealots, knowing this, focus on holding their ground and executing high-impact guerrilla strikes.

---

## The Mobius Heist

To power their competitive fleet and break the White Throne's blockade, the Zealots needed warp capability. They targeted the White Throne's proprietary warp engine: the **Mobius Drive**.

Rather than risk their own forces in a direct assault on the White Throne's shipyards, the Zealots hired **The Family** — a shadowy, professional mercenary syndicate — on a high-paying contract to steal the Drive. 

The heist itself had been predicted by the White Throne. Its algorithms had calculated every entry vector, the Family's tactics, and the defense responses perfectly. However, the White Throne failed to anticipate a single, incalculable variable: the Tatos. Because of the Tatos' spontaneous and unexpected involvement in piloting the getaway suits, the calculations broke, allowing the heist to succeed unexpectedly.

Although the drive was successfully stolen, the escape went catastrophically wrong in the aftermath, leaving the Tato pilots stranded and hunted by the White Throne's forces. How Ephraim and the Zealots first learned about the existence of the secret Mobius Drive remains one of the Crusade's unsolved mysteries.
