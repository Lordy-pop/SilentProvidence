# The Jarn
*Species file — Silent Providence*
*The tyrant natives of [HeighLunde](../heighlunde.md). An early root of the [Solitudes](../factions/the-solitudes.md).*

---

## Overview

The Jarn are the native masters of HeighLunde — an iron-proud, hierarchical people who, ages before humanity arrived, chained the world-being **Sela** and built their entire civilization on the power they wrung from its suffering. Their name comes from an old human word for *iron*: hard, cold, unyielding, the metal that would rather break than bend.

## The Iron Inheritance

To the Jarn, the chaining of Sela was never a crime. It was inheritance. The throne was passed down and the ground beneath it came with it — a thing to be used, as it always had been. Their pride was Norse in temper: vigorous, defiant, sovereign, allergic to the idea that any outside power could tell them what their own world owed them. They did not experience their tyranny as tyranny. They experienced it as *order* — theirs by right and by blood.

## Emperor Valdar

The last and greatest Jarn sovereign. His name carries an old root meaning *ruler, wielder of power*, and he is exactly that: the will that refused to release Sela, crystallized. When the refusal became the corruption, Valdar became its mind and its face. To the survivors of HeighLunde he is the mastermind — the thing at the center of the Calamity. To kill him is to break the chains. *(See [../heighlunde.md](../heighlunde.md).)*

## "Your Grace Is an Invasion"

When humanity moved to free Sela, the Jarn heard not mercy but conquest: the stranger declaring their oldest law a crime, reaching to tear the foundation out of their world and calling the theft *kindness*. And they were not simply wrong. The human grace really had come bound to human control; the uplift really had scaled their sin into something planetary; the liberation really would have unmade the world they knew. The grace was real **and** the grievance was real. That is the knot HeighLunde refuses to cut.

## The Refusal Made Flesh

The Jarn did not lose a war. They refused to open their hands, and the refusal itself became the catastrophe. The corruption that eats HeighLunde is the Jarn's collective will to keep the throne, grown into a living thing. The species and the sickness are one and the same.

## Root of the Solitudes

The Jarn's cry — *your grace is an invasion, your compassion a contagion, leave us to our own law* — is the oldest ancestor of the **Solitudes**, the later alien faction that rejects the entire human framework and names grace itself a pathogen. The Jarn never theorized it; they only lived it, on one planet, and were consumed by it. Generations on, others would make the same argument coldly, deliberately, across the galaxy. *(See [../factions/the-solitudes.md](../factions/the-solitudes.md).)*

## What We Don't Know Yet

- The Jarn's own name for Sela, for HeighLunde, for themselves.
- Whether any Jarn broke with the refusal — a dissenter, a first defector, a different line.
- Whether Valdar was always the hardest of them, or became so under human pressure.
- What the Jarn were, before they found something to enslave.
